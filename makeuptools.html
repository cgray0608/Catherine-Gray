Makeup Tools


The tools used to apply the makeup are going to have a large effect on how the "final product" turns out. Below is a quick breakdown on different tools, brushes, and what they are typically used for. The great thing about makeup and makeup tools is that you can really do whatever you want! If a brush is designed to be a lip brush, but you really like it for concealer, then you can use it that way. These are breakdowns on the traditional uses of these tools.



Brushes

  • Synthetic Hair Brushes


    Great to use with liquid and cream products as the type of bristle doesn't trap product.

  • Natural Hair Brushes


    These are great to use for powders, and give a great color payoff.

  • Small Fluffy Brushes


    These small fluffy brushes are great for blending out harsh lines.

  • Small Flat Brushes


    Can be used for many things. These are great for packing shadow on the lid, and can also be used for concealer and as a lipbrush depending on the size.

  • Dense Brushes


    Best used to blend liquid foundation, or to blend out cream products such as a cream bronzer.

  • Large Fluffy Brushes


    Large fluffy brushes are incredibly versatile. These brushes can be used to apply face powder, blush, and bronzer, and can be used to blend everything together as well.

Sponges

  • Makeup Sponges


    Makeup sponges can be used for blending liquid foundation, concealer, and cream products as well.

  • Benefits


    The main benefits associated with makeup sponges is the natural look they give. These moist sponges allow for a very natural texture when applying makeup.

  • Which is Best?

    Not all makeup sponges are created equally. There are many makeup sponges that end up absorbing up all product. It is best to do research and look at reviews before deciding what sponge to get.

Silicone Applicators

  • A Fad


    This type of makeup applicator was one that became a very big trend.

  • Effectiveness


    Many people did not like these types of applicators as they felt it really just smeared the makeup, and didn't blend it at all.

Other

  • Fingers

    A tried and true method of makeup application is using ones fingers. This can work great for certain eyeshadows, as well as for blending in liquid foundation. This reduced the possibility of any stroke marks you could get from a brush.
